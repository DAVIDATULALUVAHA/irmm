Intial Post

According to Glisson et al (2015) the major threats and vulnerabilities discussed are brute force attacks, cloning and physical attacks, medical identity theft, interception, eavesdropping, denial of service (DoS) attack and security control attacks. Kaspersky Lab (2021) defines brute force attacks as the use of extreme force to get access to private account by predicting authentication information, cipher keys or hidden content through trial and error method. Norton life lock & Steve (Weisman 2021) defines DoS attack as holding website’s resources at ransom to prevent users’ access to the same.

To mitigate against cyber threats, Halperin et al (2015) suggest cooperation between device manufacturers, cyber security experts and medical specialists to create better guidelines and hardware. Gollakota et. al., (2011) suggest the development of non- intrusive medical hardware that safeguard the hardware from outgoing and incoming signals and Diallo et. Al (2014) support creation of wireless solutions that have quicker query processing times. Kaspersky Lab (2021) suggests Salt the hash, Two-factor authentication, Password sensitization, High encryption rates and Account shut down after excessive login attempts as some of the solutions to mitigate brute force attack. Norton life lock& Steve (Weisman 2021) suggests using hardware that scans packets and blocks malicious packets and employing anti- distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) technology as a shield.

Peer Response one Posted by Kin Wong

Besides traditional security threats, I think other potential security risk maybe involved, if put the experiment to the real world.One of them is phishing. As the users may use their devices for more than just medical, such as other social media web sites, chatting software or emails. It is possible for hackers to pretend a person whom they know, send them any malicious link or file, and gain the control of the device (Phishing.org, 2021). Hackers can do anything to the medical system after having controlled the administrative device.

The other one is the Data Loss. Every data packet, which is transferred either through the cable or air, may contain individual sensible information. In The scenario, after the hacker has cracked into the Wi-Fi network, it is possible for him/her to capture the data packets and extract the data. The loss of data can be a real time health data, can be the patient records which are exchanging between devices, or any sensitivity data which as related to the patient’s confidential medical details. (ICO, 2021)

In conclusion, the attack of medical systems not only for disruption, many hackers want to steal valuable medical information, from medical records to the formula of drugs.
